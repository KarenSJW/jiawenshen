A designer without hand-drawing skill

Studied in Psychology, experience in marketing, game, user research and App design, now I'm working for SAAS web design & developing.

Started from game

Different from the "real" designers, I didn't get any design or hand drawing skills from school nor my early life. Instead, I entered the "design" field as a game designer, — after 2-years marketing job — working on gameplay and mechanism design in an MMO(Massively multiplayer online) game inventor. This is a crucial period for me to establish the skill of flow design, spec writing and UI layout design, meanwhile learned what is "develop" and how it worked in real life.

User research and startup experience

Thereafter I came back to my origin field, the user research job in an ODM company for computer accessories, planned and executed several user research campaigns there. Then I entered a startup, finally truly hands-on "design", mainly on App design and few website projects. The startup company always need to make a living, so we adopted the latest dev & design tools, pursuing agility and efficiency. Therefrom I became a real designer without hand-sketch skill.

For the present


Designer in all aspects

Now I serving for a software company, building web-based SAAS service for professional field users. Here we have no chance to become our users, neither experience and think like them, like in any other consumer-based company. So before getting to work on the design, we need to get totally understand of our users, therefore we initiated comprehensive research activities, built UX materials including persona, user journey...etc., in the end, brings out the flowchart, wireframe, UI design, and prototype. Sometimes I'd even make some code trial myself for demo and better communication.

Sharing my own experience and skills

Recently another important task is to assist in integrating the design department. I'm trying to organize the team by introducing Design process. For now, my team and I are working together building an operative style guideline, and I'll try to proceed a design workflow, for sharing my experience on UX like requirement tracking, user research, and usability testing.
